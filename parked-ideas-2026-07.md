Ideas raised and deliberately parked while stepping back from millefleur to

## millefleur sub-projects (iterations on the live piece)

- **Cyclical millefleur via death of immortals.** One new rule turns the
  terminating artwork into an endless slow kaleidoscope. Candidate death
  rules considered:
  - **Decay of the fruitless (Greg likes this one).** A flower persists
    only while its lineage keeps succeeding; a flower whose descendants
    all fail (no successful immortalization descended from it in a long
    window) fades and frees its space. Persistence = ongoing consequence.
    Mystical reading: you exist as long as you matter to the future.
  - **Age-based death** — conveyor belt; erases deep history; parked as
    the boring option.
  - **Displacement (REJECTED — degenerate).** "A newcomer that fully
    unfolds kills what it overlapped" requires ghost-growth through
    immortals, which removes the only obstacle that makes unfolding a
    fitness test — nearly every seed would succeed; total churn. Rescuing
    it needs handicap rules (only displace smaller/older, must create
    more than it kills), i.e. rule-stacking. Greg spotted the flaw.
- **Differential reproduction / emission weighting.** Currently seeds come
  from a uniformly random immortal, so there is no selection after
  immortalization. Weight emission by size, frontier-adjacency, recency,
  or descendant success — real fitness gradients with zero new state.
- **Upward growth bias.** Give growth (or germination, or dispersal) a
  vertical asymmetry so forms tend up-and-out. Note: "up" currently does
  not exist anywhere in the ontology (random facing at germination).
  Related: Fra Angelico 3/4 perspective (see millefleur-HANDOFF.md open
  decision #3).
- **Failure residue.** Failed plants currently vanish tracelessly — the
  world only remembers winners. Let failures leave a faint residue that
  biases future germination/growth (environment-as-urn, smallest possible
  step toward environment-encodes-history).
- **UI: arbitrarily large canvas + zoom/pan exploration.** Decouple world
  size from window; explore a huge completed tapestry.
- **1 pixel = 1 cell mode.** Maximum-density render mode.
- **Environmental fitness.** Spatial variation (moisture/light bands or
  similar) so different regions select different forms — environmentally
  based adaptation instead of purely packing-based selection.
- **Mutation-count distribution, dispersal as a genetic trait,
  completion-detection tuning** (carried over from millefleur-HANDOFF-2).

## Cross-project observations worth keeping (from the catalog read)

- The whole 5.5-year catalog oscillates between **physics-worlds**
  (matter, transport, conservation; no identity or lineage) and
  **genetics-worlds** (heredity, selection; blank passive grids). Every
  attempted marriage (absorption, cellspring, evo-engine Phase 1,
  matter-plants) died of complexity. Millefleur succeeded by going pure
  genetics-pole. The open frontier: the marriage at millefleur-level
  austerity — one law, no economy.
- **Buried gems:** urn-plants' path-keyed urns (identity as sedimented
  habit — "history becomes destiny"); sunsweeper's celestial sweep (time
  arrives as a place; cheapest possible environment); pixiching/rbgrps
  (the directed 3-cycle as the minimum unit of perpetual aliveness).
- Millefleur is the only project with **no cycle** — an arrow that
  terminates. That's why it reads as artwork, not ecology. Reintroducing
  any cycle changes its genre.
- The candidate junction for the radically-new project: **a world where
  matter and lineage are the same substance** — bodies made of something
  that must come from somewhere and return somewhere — held to
  millefleur's austerity.
