# Parked ideas — brainstorming session 2026-07-08

Ideas raised and deliberately parked while stepping back from millefleur to
look for something radically new. None of these are commitments; they're
recovery points. Context: `millefleur-HANDOFF-2.md`, `millefleur-HANDOFF.md`,
`colorless-green-ideas.md`, `PROJECT-CATALOG.md`.

## millefleur sub-projects (iterations on the live piece)

- **Cyclical millefleur via death of immortals.** One new rule turns the
  terminating artwork into an endless slow kaleidoscope. Candidate death
  rules considered:
  - **Decay of the fruitless (Greg likes this one).** A flower persists
    only while its lineage keeps succeeding; a flower whose descendants
    all fail (no successful immortalization descended from it in a long
    window) fades and frees its space. Persistence = ongoing consequence.
    Mystical reading: you exist as long as you matter to the future.
  - **Age-based death** — conveyor belt; erases deep history; parked as
    the boring option.
  - **Displacement (REJECTED — degenerate).** "A newcomer that fully
    unfolds kills what it overlapped" requires ghost-growth through
    immortals, which removes the only obstacle that makes unfolding a
    fitness test — nearly every seed would succeed; total churn. Rescuing
    it needs handicap rules (only displace smaller/older, must create
    more than it kills), i.e. rule-stacking. Greg spotted the flaw.
- **Differential reproduction / emission weighting.** Currently seeds come
  from a uniformly random immortal, so there is no selection after
  immortalization. Weight emission by size, frontier-adjacency, recency,
  or descendant success — real fitness gradients with zero new state.
- **Upward growth bias.** Give growth (or germination, or dispersal) a
  vertical asymmetry so forms tend up-and-out. Note: "up" currently does
  not exist anywhere in the ontology (random facing at germination).
  Related: Fra Angelico 3/4 perspective (see millefleur-HANDOFF.md open
  decision #3).
- **Failure residue.** Failed plants currently vanish tracelessly — the
  world only remembers winners. Let failures leave a faint residue that
  biases future germination/growth (environment-as-urn, smallest possible
  step toward environment-encodes-history).
- **UI: arbitrarily large canvas + zoom/pan exploration.** Decouple world
  size from window; explore a huge completed tapestry.
- **1 pixel = 1 cell mode.** Maximum-density render mode.
- **Environmental fitness.** Spatial variation (moisture/light bands or
  similar) so different regions select different forms — environmentally
  based adaptation instead of purely packing-based selection.
- **Mutation-count distribution, dispersal as a genetic trait,
  completion-detection tuning** (carried over from millefleur-HANDOFF-2).

## Cross-project observations worth keeping (from the catalog read)

- The whole 5.5-year catalog oscillates between **physics-worlds**
  (matter, transport, conservation; no identity or lineage) and
  **genetics-worlds** (heredity, selection; blank passive grids). Every
  attempted marriage (absorption, cellspring, evo-engine Phase 1,
  matter-plants) died of complexity. Millefleur succeeded by going pure
  genetics-pole. The open frontier: the marriage at millefleur-level
  austerity — one law, no economy.
- **Buried gems:** urn-plants' path-keyed urns (identity as sedimented
  habit — "history becomes destiny"); sunsweeper's celestial sweep (time
  arrives as a place; cheapest possible environment); pixiching/rbgrps
  (the directed 3-cycle as the minimum unit of perpetual aliveness).
- Millefleur is the only project with **no cycle** — an arrow that
  terminates. That's why it reads as artwork, not ecology. Reintroducing
  any cycle changes its genre.
- The candidate junction for the radically-new project: **a world where
  matter and lineage are the same substance** — bodies made of something
  that must come from somewhere and return somewhere — held to
  millefleur's austerity.

## The live candidate: "matter as a loan, corpse as ground" (Greg likes it)

One conserved pool — **substance**. The world starts as substance. A form
grows by SPENDING substance from the cells it occupies into its body; it
cannot grow where nothing is left to spend. Death doesn't vanish the body —
the body BECOMES substrate again, deposited where it fell. No energy, no
light, no income/upkeep economy. One law: **to live is to borrow matter
into a shape; to die is to return it, somewhere.**

Why it's austere: conservation is automatic (nothing created), selection is
automatic (you can only grow where enough has been returned), environment-
encodes-history is automatic (the ground IS the accumulated dead), and it
cycles forever (death refills the pool). Not an arrow toward a finished
tapestry — a slow tide of matter sloshing between free and embodied, with
lineages riding it.

**Open fork (Greg wants to see BOTH):**
- **Austere:** substrate sits inert where it's dropped. No physics engine.
  Growth patterns alone move matter around the world over generations.
- **Alive:** substrate also moves (diffuses, settles, flows downhill under
  gravity). More alive, but reintroduces a physics layer — the thing that
  killed absorption/cellspring/matter-plants. If tried, it must stay ONE
  rule (e.g. bare settling), not an economy.

Unresolved design questions parked with it: what "death" is in this world
(millefleur-style failure-to-unfold? decay of the fruitless? something
else); whether genomes ride on seeds (simplant-style) or live in the
ground (urn-in-the-substrate, path-keyed); what a "seed" even carries if
bodies are loans.

## STRATA — the artwork is the core sample (Greg likes it)

Side view. The world only ever ACCRETES: when a form dies, its body
compresses into ground where it stood and the ground level rises. Life is
a thin bright film at the surface; everything below is permanent, readable
history — lineage colors and shapes flattened into geology like tree rings
or an ice core. You scroll DOWN to read the deep past; the UI is
archaeology. The piece never completes: the deliverable isn't the final
frame, it's the whole geological column.

- **Topography emerges from success.** Corpses pile where lineages thrive,
  so life literally builds hills where it flourishes and leaves basins
  where it fails — and then must live on the terrain its ancestors became.
  Selection pressure drifts over geological time for free.
- Conservation trivial (corpse → stratum). Sun energy enters only at the
  surface. Environment-encodes-history taken to the limit: the environment
  eventually IS nothing but history.
- Camera can rise with the surface — the piece is a column that grows
  forever; the past sinks away below.
- Open/parked: erosion or compaction rules; bounded width vs. infinite
  scroll; whether strata stay inert (austere) or can be re-mined by roots.

## THE TWO KINGDOMS — co-evolution as a matter cycle (Greg's favorite)

The minimum unit of perpetual aliveness is a directed cycle (rbgrps/
pixiching insight). Smallest living version: two interleaved kingdoms,
each running millefleur's exact law (fully unfold or vanish), but **each
can only grow on what the other's dead leave behind.** Matter cycles
through four states — ash → flower(A body) → loam → mycelium(B body) →
ash — two living, two dead. No energy, no upkeep: unfold-or-die, twice,
pointed at each other.

- **Co-evolution is structural, not decorative.** Each kingdom IS the
  other's environment; every adaptation in one rewrites the other's
  fitness landscape. Mutual arising, mechanically true.
- **Failure feeds the cycle.** Unlike millefleur's traceless failures, a
  blocked plant's matter converts FORWARD (aborted A becomes loam anyway) —
  the world remembers failures as food. Nothing is wasted; even the losers
  nourish the other kingdom.
- **Drama for free.** If A booms, it locks matter into A-bodies → a feast
  accumulates for B → B booms → A starves → oscillation. Predator-prey
  dynamics with zero predation mechanics, purely from conservation.
- **Shapes echo shapes.** B can only grow where A died, so B's morphology
  is a distorted trace of A's ghosts, and vice versa forever — two
  tapestries interleaved, each the negative of the other's past. Two
  palettes (warm/cool) would make this legible.
- Open: what triggers death of a mature form (lifespan? decay of the
  fruitless? being grown-through?); seed dispersal per kingdom; whether
  kingdoms' rules are identical (symmetric) or asymmetric.

## Composition sketch: TWO KINGDOMS on STRATA (fresh bolt)

Side view. Kingdom A (plants) grows UP from the surface into air; kingdom
B (mycelium) grows DOWN beneath the surface, digesting the buried strata
of A's dead. The surface line is the membrane between two interpenetrating
worlds, each feeding on the other's dead — the underworld literally
digests history and frees matter back to the living. Needs care with how
freed matter returns up (mushroom fruiting at the surface? roots reaching
down to ash?) — complexity risk, but the image is the strongest of the
session: heaven and underworld co-evolving across a membrane that is
itself made of time.
