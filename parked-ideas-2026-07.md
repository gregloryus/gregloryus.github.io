# Parked ideas — brainstorming session 2026-07-08

Ideas raised and deliberately parked while stepping back from millefleur to
look for something radically new. None of these are commitments; they're
recovery points. Context: `millefleur-HANDOFF-2.md`, `millefleur-HANDOFF.md`,

## millefleur sub-projects (iterations on the live piece)

- **Cyclical millefleur via death of immortals.** One new rule turns the
  terminating artwork into an endless slow kaleidoscope. Candidate death
  rules considered:
  - **Decay of the fruitless (Greg likes this one).** A flower persists
    only while its lineage keeps succeeding; a flower whose descendants
    all fail (no successful immortalization descended from it in a long
    window) fades and frees its space. Persistence = ongoing consequence.
    Mystical reading: you exist as long as you matter to the future.
  - **Age-based death** — conveyor belt; erases deep history; parked as
    the boring option.
  - **Displacement (REJECTED — degenerate).** "A newcomer that fully
    unfolds kills what it overlapped" requires ghost-growth through
    immortals, which removes the only obstacle that makes unfolding a
    fitness test — nearly every seed would succeed; total churn. Rescuing
    it needs handicap rules (only displace smaller/older, must create
    more than it kills), i.e. rule-stacking. Greg spotted the flaw.
- **Differential reproduction / emission weighting.** Currently seeds come
  from a uniformly random immortal, so there is no selection after
  immortalization. Weight emission by size, frontier-adjacency, recency,
  or descendant success — real fitness gradients with zero new state.
- **Upward growth bias.** Give growth (or germination, or dispersal) a
  vertical asymmetry so forms tend up-and-out. Note: "up" currently does
  not exist anywhere in the ontology (random facing at germination).
  Related: Fra Angelico 3/4 perspective (see millefleur-HANDOFF.md open
  decision #3).
- **Failure residue.** Failed plants currently vanish tracelessly — the
  world only remembers winners. Let failures leave a faint residue that
  biases future germination/growth (environment-as-urn, smallest possible
  step toward environment-encodes-history).
- **UI: arbitrarily large canvas + zoom/pan exploration.** Decouple world
  size from window; explore a huge completed tapestry.
- **1 pixel = 1 cell mode.** Maximum-density render mode.
- **Environmental fitness.** Spatial variation (moisture/light bands or
  similar) so different regions select different forms — environmentally
  based adaptation instead of purely packing-based selection.
- **Mutation-count distribution, dispersal as a genetic trait,
  completion-detection tuning** (carried over from millefleur-HANDOFF-2).

## The live candidate: "matter as a loan, corpse as ground" (Greg likes it)

One conserved pool — **substance**. The world starts as substance. A form
grows by SPENDING substance from the cells it occupies into its body; it
cannot grow where nothing is left to spend. Death doesn't vanish the body —
the body BECOMES substrate again, deposited where it fell. No energy, no
light, no income/upkeep economy. One law: **to live is to borrow matter
into a shape; to die is to return it, somewhere.**

Why it's austere: conservation is automatic (nothing created), selection is
automatic (you can only grow where enough has been returned), environment-
encodes-history is automatic (the ground IS the accumulated dead), and it
cycles forever (death refills the pool). Not an arrow toward a finished
tapestry — a slow tide of matter sloshing between free and embodied, with
lineages riding it.

**Open fork (Greg wants to see BOTH):**
- **Austere:** substrate sits inert where it's dropped. No physics engine.
  Growth patterns alone move matter around the world over generations.
- **Alive:** substrate also moves (diffuses, settles, flows downhill under
  gravity). More alive, but reintroduces a physics layer — the thing that
  killed absorption/cellspring/matter-plants. If tried, it must stay ONE
  rule (e.g. bare settling), not an economy.

Unresolved design questions parked with it: what "death" is in this world
(millefleur-style failure-to-unfold? decay of the fruitless? something
else); whether genomes ride on seeds (simplant-style) or live in the
ground (urn-in-the-substrate, path-keyed); what a "seed" even carries if
bodies are loans.

## STRATA — the artwork is the core sample (Greg likes it)

Side view. The world only ever ACCRETES: when a form dies, its body
compresses into ground where it stood and the ground level rises. Life is
a thin bright film at the surface; everything below is permanent, readable
history — lineage colors and shapes flattened into geology like tree rings
or an ice core. You scroll DOWN to read the deep past; the UI is
archaeology. The piece never completes: the deliverable isn't the final
frame, it's the whole geological column.

- **Topography emerges from success.** Corpses pile where lineages thrive,
  so life literally builds hills where it flourishes and leaves basins
  where it fails — and then must live on the terrain its ancestors became.
  Selection pressure drifts over geological time for free.
- Conservation trivial (corpse → stratum). Sun energy enters only at the
  surface. Environment-encodes-history taken to the limit: the environment
  eventually IS nothing but history.
- Camera can rise with the surface — the piece is a column that grows
  forever; the past sinks away below.
- Open/parked: erosion or compaction rules; bounded width vs. infinite
  scroll; whether strata stay inert (austere) or can be re-mined by roots.

## THE TWO KINGDOMS — co-evolution as a matter cycle (Greg's favorite)

The minimum unit of perpetual aliveness is a directed cycle (rbgrps/
pixiching insight). Smallest living version: two interleaved kingdoms,
each running millefleur's exact law (fully unfold or vanish), but **each
can only grow on what the other's dead leave behind.** Matter cycles
through four states — ash → flower(A body) → loam → mycelium(B body) →
ash — two living, two dead. No energy, no upkeep: unfold-or-die, twice,
pointed at each other.

- **Co-evolution is structural, not decorative.** Each kingdom IS the
  other's environment; every adaptation in one rewrites the other's
  fitness landscape. Mutual arising, mechanically true.
- **Failure feeds the cycle.** Unlike millefleur's traceless failures, a
  blocked plant's matter converts FORWARD (aborted A becomes loam anyway) —
  the world remembers failures as food. Nothing is wasted; even the losers
  nourish the other kingdom.
- **Drama for free.** If A booms, it locks matter into A-bodies → a feast
  accumulates for B → B booms → A starves → oscillation. Predator-prey
  dynamics with zero predation mechanics, purely from conservation.
- **Shapes echo shapes.** B can only grow where A died, so B's morphology
  is a distorted trace of A's ghosts, and vice versa forever — two
  tapestries interleaved, each the negative of the other's past. Two
  palettes (warm/cool) would make this legible.
- Open: what triggers death of a mature form (lifespan? decay of the
  fruitless? being grown-through?); seed dispersal per kingdom; whether
  kingdoms' rules are identical (symmetric) or asymmetric.

## Composition sketch: TWO KINGDOMS on STRATA (fresh bolt)

Side view. Kingdom A (plants) grows UP from the surface into air; kingdom
B (mycelium) grows DOWN beneath the surface, digesting the buried strata
of A's dead. The surface line is the membrane between two interpenetrating
worlds, each feeding on the other's dead — the underworld literally
digests history and frees matter back to the living. Needs care with how
freed matter returns up (mushroom fruiting at the surface? roots reaching
down to ash?) — complexity risk, but the image is the strongest of the
session: heaven and underworld co-evolving across a membrane that is
itself made of time.

## Fresh-instance wave (2026-07-08, later session): three bodies

Greg's verdict: fine/interesting, parked; still too constrained (one-law
and side-view were self-imposed, not required). Each varies "what is a
body?"

- **WATERSHED — rivers with lineage.** One substance (sediment), one law
  (water flows downhill; fast water erodes, slow water deposits; rain is
  the energy input). A body is a drainage channel — not the water, the
  shape the water keeps agreeing to take. Reproduction = headward
  branching; predation/co-evolution = stream capture; death =
  abandonment, buried as terraces. The urn-erosion isomorphism promoted
  to entire ontology: terrain is nothing but accumulated deeds. Flag:
  heredity is habit-shaped, not genome-shaped — evolution or just
  beautiful physics is the live question.
- **EMBER — beings are events, not objects.** Nothing ever moves. Per
  cell: charge (slow ambient trickle = sun) and memory. One law: a firing
  cell dumps charge into neighbors weighted by their memory; firing
  deposits memory; memory fades. A being is a traveling discharge front;
  its genome is the memory trail (environment-as-urn as the whole world);
  reproduction = one fire lighting another. No transport at all — the
  physics layer that killed absorption/cellspring simply doesn't exist.
  Forgetting is built in. Beings are verbs; the world is a noun that
  remembers being verbed. (Answers pixiching's empty updateFLAME(): fire
  isn't a particle, it's what happens to particles — here everything
  alive is that kind of thing.)
- **THE LOOM — conservation of thread.** Fixed population of unbroken
  1-cell-wide threads, each with permanent color/identity, never created
  or destroyed; extend at the head, unravel at the tail (length
  conserved). A being is a braid — threads interlocked into a weave
  stable enough to hold them; growth = snaring passing threads; death =
  unraveling, threads freed unharmed into other lives. Matter and lineage
  are the same object. Interpenetration becomes physical fact: cut any
  being open and find strands that were part of other beings. Tapestry
  promoted from aesthetic to ontology. Highest complexity risk (how a
  pattern re-weaves a copy of itself is open — rhymes with DNA→protein
  templating), highest ceiling.

Bolt noticed while writing: EMBER at steady state becomes a RHYTHM — a
memory loop that re-burns periodically is an oscillator. Recurring fires
= beings with a beat. Connects forward to the rhythm-ecology idea in the
next wave.

## Second fresh-instance wave: varying time and observer

- **PULSE — an ecology of rhythms.** Every cell is a tiny oscillator
  (charge slowly, flash, nudge neighbors' timing — firefly/Kuramoto
  coupling). A being is a RHYTHM occupying cells the way a wave occupies
  water; its genome is pure timing (interval, phase offsets). Reproduction
  = entrainment: converting neighbors to your beat is your lineage
  spreading. Perfect synchrony must be self-defeating (synced regions
  exhaust local charge) so the world never settles — permanent chimera
  weather of sync and chaos, empires of rhythm expanding and fraying.
  Heredity of WHEN instead of WHAT. The hypnosis is literal: the viewer's
  visual system entrains to the same law the sim runs on. One phase value
  per cell — cheap as dirt. (Render bolt: hue = phase, so sync waves are
  rainbow spirals and a lineage's beat is visible as a traveling color.)
- **GAZE GARDEN — attention is the sunlight (GREG'S FAVORITE of this
  wave).** The world's only energy input is being looked at: a soft
  radial field around the viewer's focus (cursor or viewport center).
  Life blooms where you linger, over-blooms, exhausts, needs you to move
  on; the terrain consolidates as a heatmap of everywhere you've ever
  looked — the world is a portrait of your own attention, walkable like
  ruins. The load-bearing bolt: fitness = holding your gaze. Boring
  lineages lose the light; interesting ones keep photosynthesizing.
  Sexual selection with the viewer as chooser — the peacock's tail, and
  you are the peahen. The sim evolves toward whatever hypnotizes YOU;
  every viewer breeds a different garden. Crucially, "interesting" is
  never defined anywhere in the code — the selection for beauty is
  implicit, uncoded, supplied entirely by the human in the loop.
  - Bolt while writing: a session's gaze track is a SEED. Replay a
    recorded gaze → deterministic regrowth of that exact garden. Gardens
    become pure functions of a history of attention — you could plant
    someone else's month of looking and watch what it grows. Sharing a
    garden = sharing where you looked.

## Third fresh-instance wave: varying the container itself

Prior waves varied what fills the world. These vary the world's laws,
its knowing, and its shape. Each: one substrate, one law, zero economy.

- **CANON — the laws of physics compete for territory.** Each cell:
  one bit of state + a tiny RULE (its own personal physics, a few bytes).
  Cells run their own law; occasionally a cell adopts the rule of a
  neighbor whose region is livelier (one simple vitality measure — more
  state-change, less frozen, less noise). What evolves is the laws
  themselves: rules generating persistent self-renewing texture hold
  territory; rules that freeze or boil get overwritten at their borders.
  Screen = map of competing physics, each region visibly a different
  fabric of reality; all drama at the frontiers. Genome = physics.
  Millefleur asks "what shape survives in this world"; CANON asks "what
  WORLD survives."
- **AUGUR — to exist is to expect.** Each cell: one bit of state + a tiny
  predictor ("given my neighborhood now, what will it be next tick").
  Correct prediction persists; repeated surprise fades; a faded cell
  restarts with its best-predicting neighbor's model. One law: anticipate
  or dissolve. Organisms are not defined anywhere — an organism IS an
  island of mutual predictability in an unpredictable sea (close to a
  real theoretical-biology definition, here made the literal mechanic).
  Boundaries of beings are visible as where prediction fails — surprise
  is the edge of every being. Co-evolution automatic: your neighbor is
  part of what you must predict, so every adaptation anywhere betrays
  everyone's model of you. Nothing conserved except understanding.
- **KNIT — space itself is the organism.** Drop the grid: cells + LINKS,
  and the links are what change. One Hebbian law: links between cells
  whose states track each other strengthen; uncorrelated links dissolve;
  a link-starved cell reaches out to a neighbor-of-a-neighbor. Contents
  nearly nothing (a blinking bit). What grows/competes/reproduces is
  GEOGRAPHY: tight clumps that behave as one, bridges, continents of
  connectivity tearing along weak seams. "Who touches whom" is the
  genome; distance is descent — nearness is shared history of resonance
  made literal. Beings aren't IN space affecting each other; the
  affecting is what space IS. Render risk: force-directed mud.

## INDRA'S NET — the final distillation (the one-sentence world)

The Huayan jewel-net as a machine. One rule, one sentence:

**Each moment, every cell becomes a copy of the one neighbor its current
color points to** (hue read as direction around the compass), plus a rare
whisper of hue drift. No self-term in the update. No other state.

Why each doctrine is mechanical, not decorative:

- **No hidden state:** the screen IS the simulation; every cause is a
  visible neighbor; the rule fits in a sentence a viewer can watch
  operating.
- **Anatta / impermanence:** you cannot hold yourself in existence for
  one tick; your color survives only if a neighbor's disposition takes
  it up. Decay-of-the-fruitless radicalized to every cell, every tick.
- **Emptiness with lineage:** every pixel is a copy of a copy back to
  frame one — lineage isn't tracked, it's what the image is. Hue is also
  the disposition (which neighbor you take up), so inheriting a color =
  inheriting a way of relating. No essence, only genealogy of influence.
- **Selection for free:** hues whose directions form closed loops of
  mutual taking-up are BEINGS — standing vortices of reflection,
  whirlpools persisting while every drop passes through. Outward-pointing
  hues scatter and die. Mutation proposes new dispositions forever.
- **Living frontier:** coherent interiors go calm (local equilibrium);
  all life is at borders where two grammars of interdependence contest
  every cell. Never closes — equilibrium is one mutation from a new
  whirlpool.
- **Engineering:** one Uint8Array of hues; millions of cells; seeded;
  pure copying conserves the palette exactly, so blur-to-mud death of
  averaging CAs is structurally impossible — variance can only
  reorganize, never dissipate. ONE tuning knob: drift rate (too low =
  eternal crystal, too high = boil; between = the living regime).
- **The fusion answer:** there was never a matter-pole and lineage-pole
  to fuse — matter IS lineage once a thing's substance is nothing but its
  history of being copied.

Bolts while writing it down:

- **The minimal being is two jewels facing.** Two adjacent cells whose
  hues point at each other = a period-2 loop of mutual gift — the
  smallest possible entity, and it's a RELATIONSHIP, not a thing.
- **Beings are rhythms — PULSE is inside this.** Every copy-loop of
  period N is an oscillator; a being's cycle period is its heartbeat.
  The rhythm-ecology idea isn't a sibling project, it's an emergent
  layer of this one.
- **Gauge symmetry: the palette is the physics.** Adding a constant to
  every hue rotates every direction by the same angle — a global hue
  shift is a WIND blowing through the whole world. Palette rotation is
  physically meaningful, not cosmetic; slow ambient hue rotation would
  act as a global season without adding any state.
- **Genesis is a mass extinction.** Start from pure noise (maximal
  difference): the first epoch is the visible dying-off of every
  unviable disposition, resolving into the first vortices — creation
  myth included free of charge.
- **Torus topology** — no edges, no up; the net has no border and needs
  none.
